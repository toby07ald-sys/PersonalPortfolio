Project Management: Agile & Kanban

Explanation: In professional software development, coding is only one part of the process; managing the workflow is equally important. Agile is the industry-standard approach for building software iteratively. Using frameworks like Kanban allows developers to visualize tasks, limit work-in-progress, and adapt quickly to changing requirements, ensuring that projects are delivered efficiently and without errors.

Context: I apply Agile methodologies to my academic and personal projects to manage complex workflows. Specifically, I utilize Kanban boards to break down large requirements into manageable tasks. For my NCTX Bus Ticketing project, I used this iterative approach to develop and test individual features—such as the authentication logic—before integrating them into the final build. This ensured systematic development and met customer expectations.

Evidence: Use of GitHub Projects (Kanban) for the NCTX system development.
